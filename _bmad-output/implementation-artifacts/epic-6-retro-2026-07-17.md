## What Went Well

- **The Epic-4/5 lessons were applied, and they caught real things.** Every story opened with
  Task 0 story-start verification (epic-5 item 4). 6.2's live-wire probe caught the **httpx
  auto-decompression trap** before any code was written (`aiter_raw` vs `aiter_bytes` — the byte
  ceiling would have silently measured the 579 MB decoded stream instead of the 26 MB wire gzip);
  6.3's baseline check caught the 6.2 close note being **off by one** (1,219 documented vs 1,220
  collected). "Verify, don't trust" demonstrably pays for itself.
- **The write-path checklist got its first real exercise and passed.** 6.1 (AC 6) enumerated all
  11 `deck_cards` write paths with per-path dispositions — the Epic-4 "end-to-end field" failure
  class did not recur on any write path.
- **The frozen contracts held under real-world drift.** Spellbook renamed its bracket vocabulary
  since the spine was written (Ruthless/Spicy/Powerful/Oddball/Core/Exhibition on the wire vs the
  six frozen `ComboBracketTag` tokens). Resolved with a **closed letter→token map** at import —
  semantically lossless (Spellbook's own derived bracket numbers coincide exactly), no Literal
  reopening, `B`/banned skipped+counted, anything unknown a loud abort. The 5.6 "no fuzzy
  fallback" rule worked as designed.
- **The `name_keys` relocation (epic-5 item 9) closed the DFC hazard cleanly.** One shared
  normalization now serves the matcher, the importer's piece index, and the repository's relevance
  filter; all 46 matcher tests passed untouched as the relocation proof. The hazard that bit 5.3,
  5.6, and 5.9 did not recur.
- **Real-data validation exceeded expectations.** 6.3's optional benchmark re-run swapped the
  hand-built cEDH fixtures for real snapshot variants: zero divergences across all 6 Commander
  entries, and the over-fetch design held at real scale (1.1k–5.3k candidate variants per deck,
  trimmed by the matcher to single/double digits). The hand-built fixtures were faithful.
- **The quality curve kept rising:** 2 Patch → 6 Low → clean review, story over story. Nothing
  above Low severity in the entire epic.

## Challenges & Lessons

### 1. The checklist's blind spot — result constructors

6.1's review found the commander flag **silently dropped in a third constructor**: the
`DatabaseError` fallback path's `DeckImportLineResult(...)` passed `sideboard` but omitted
`commander`. Exactly the failure class the write-path checklist targets — one enumeration surface
over. Write paths were fully enumerated; *result constructors* were not. Report-only impact
(nothing persisted wrong), but the class is the same as Epic 4's original miss.

**Lesson → action item:** when a field threads through multiple constructors, grep **every
construction site** — including error/fallback-path result objects — before claiming end-to-end.
6.2 and 6.3 already carried this forward in their story notes ("enumerate every construction site
of what you touch").

### 2. Upstream vocabulary drift is a fact of life

The Spellbook bracket-tag rename was absorbed without contract damage, but only because the
import boundary had a decide-once normalization policy and a hard-error path for unknowns. The
generalizable rule: external vocabularies drift; the closed-map + loud-abort pattern at the import
boundary is the correct insurance and should be the default for any future wire ingestion.

### 3. Calibration honesty carried over — now scheduled as a gate

The green benchmark (now on real combo data) still proves scoring *shape* on ten
reconstructed decks, not real-world validity. Epic-5 action item 5 (real-deck sanity pass) was
**not addressed during Epic 6** — and Epic 7, where tuning ossifies, is next. Sathias's ruling
this retro: **escalate it to a blocking Pre-Epic-7 Gate** (same pattern as the Pre-Epic-6
Importer Gate). See Action Items.
